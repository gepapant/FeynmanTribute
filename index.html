Richard Feynman

The man behind quantum mechanics

Here's a time line of Feynman's life:

  • 1918 - Born in New York City, USA
  • 1935 - Feynman begins his undergraduate studies at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).
  • 1939 - He graduates from MIT with a bachelor's degree in mathematics
  • 1942 - Completes his doctoral dissertation titled "The Principle of Least Action in Quantum Mechanics" at Princeton University.
  • 1943-1945 - Works on the Manhattan Project at Los Alamos, contributing to the development of the atomic bomb.
  • 1940s - Introduces Feynman diagrams, revolutionizing the field of particle physics.
  • 1950s - Develops the path integral formulation of quantum mechanics.
  • 1965 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work in quantum electrodynamics.
  • 1960 - 1980 - Teaches at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), inspiring students with his dynamic lectures.
  • 1986 - Serves on the Rogers Commission investigating the Space Shuttle Challenger disaster.
  • 1986 - Feynman's book "Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!" is published, showcasing his wit and insights into science.
  • 1989 - "The Feynman Lectures on Physics," a landmark series of physics textbooks is being published after him passing away.
  • 1988 - dies at the age of 70.

When Feynman was nearing death, he asked his friend and colleague Danny Hillis why Hillis appeared so sad. Hillis replied that he thought Feynman was going to die soon. Hillis quotes Feynman as replying:

"Yeah," he sighed, "that bugs me sometimes too. But not so much as you think . . . when you get as old as I am, you start to realize that you've told most of the good stuff you know to other people anyway."
